	\paragraph{Problem}
	\begin{quote}
		\textsf{In equilateral triangles $ABC$ and
			$ADE$, $AB=3$  in and $BD = \dfrac{AB}{3}$  in. Find
			the area of the shaded triangle $CDF$.}
	\end{quote}

	% Reasoning
	\paragraph{Reasoning}
	\begin{quotation}
		
		Given that $AB=3$ and $BD=\frac{AB}{3}$, $BD=\frac{3}{3}=1$, which is denoted above. Draw $\overline{AG}$ from $A$ such that $\overline{AG} \bot \overline{BC}$; this is $\triangle ABC$'s altitude by definition (5). Given that $\triangle ABC$ and $\triangle ADE$ are equilateral triangles, their sides are congruent: $\overline{AB}\cong\overline{BC}\cong\overline{CA}$ and $\overline{AD}\cong\overline{DE}\cong\overline{EA}$; thus $AB=BC=CA$ and $AD=DE=EA$ by definition. $A$ is on the perpendicular bisector of $\overline{BC}$ by the Converse of the Perpendicular Bisector Theorem (4); therefore $\overline{AG}$ bisects $\overline{BC}$. By the definition of segment bisection, $\overline{BG}\cong\overline{GC}$ and therefore $BG=GC$. Since $\overline{BC}$ is a side of $\triangle ABC$, $BC=3$; $BG+GC=BC$ by the SAP; $BG+BG=BC$ by substitution, $2BG=BC$ by the distributive property, and $BG=\frac{3}{2}=1.5$. $BD+DG=BG$ by the SAP; with $BD=1$, $1+DG=1.5$ and $DG=1.5-1=0.5$ after subtracting both sides by 1. Since an equilateral triangle is also an equiangular triangle (3), $\text{m}\angle ABC=\text{m}\angle BCA=\text{m}\angle CAB=\text{m}\angle ADE=\text{m}\angle DEA=\text{m}\angle EAD=60$\textdegree. Since $\overline{AG}\bot\overline{BC}$, $\angle AGB$ and $\angle AGC$ are right angles. With one right angle ($\angle AGB$), $\triangle ABG$ is a right triangle, which makes it eligible for the Pythagorean Theorem (7): $a^2+b^2=c^2 \Rightarrow 1.5^2+AG^2=3^2 \Rightarrow 2.25+AG^2=9$; thus $AG^2=9-2.25=6.75$ and $AG=\sqrt{6.75}$. In right triangle $ADG$, $\text{m}\angle ADG = \tan^{-1} \frac{AG}{DG}=\tan^{-1} \frac{\sqrt{6.75}}{0.5}=\tan^{-1}2\sqrt{6.75}$. By the Angle Addition Postulate, $\text{m}\angle CDF+\text{m}\angle FDA=\text{m}\angle CDA \Rightarrow \text{m}\angle CDA=\text{m}\angle CDF+60$. $\angle ADG\cong\angle CDA$ by the Reflexive Property, and therefore m$\angle ADG=\text{m}\angle CDA=\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75}$ by the Transitive Property. Subtracting 60\textdegree \space from both sides yields $\text{m}\angle CDF=\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75}-60$\textdegree. According to the Triangle Sum Theorem (1), $\text{m}\angle CDF+\text{m}\angle DFC+\text{m}\angle DCF=180$\textdegree \space $\Rightarrow \tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75}-60$\textdegree$+\text{m}\angle DFC+60$\textdegree$=\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75}+\text{m}\angle DFC=180$\textdegree $\Rightarrow \text{m}\angle DFC=180-\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75}$. By the SAP, $BD+DC=BC \Rightarrow 1+DC=3 \Rightarrow DC=3-1=2$. By the Law of Sines (6), $\frac{\sin \angle DFC}{DC}=\frac{\sin \angle DCF}{DF} \Rightarrow \frac{\sin (180-\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75})}{2}=\frac{\sin 60\text{\textdegree}}{DF}$; by the Cross Product Property, $DF\sin (180-\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75})=2 \sin 60\text{\textdegree} \Rightarrow DF=\frac{2 \sin 60\text{\textdegree}}{\sin (180-\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75})}$. The exact value of $\sin 60$\textdegree \space can be determined using the properties of a 30\textdegree-60\textdegree-90\textdegree \space triangle to be $\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$; as such, $DF=\frac{2 \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}}{\sin (180-\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75})}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{\sin (180-\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75})}$. Draw $FH$ from $F$ perpendicular to $\overline{DC}$; this serves as the altitude of $\triangle CDF$ by definition (5). Because $\overline{FH}$ is perpendicular to $\overline{DC}$, $\angle DHF$ is a right angle and $\triangle DHF$ is a right triangle. $\sin \angle CDF=\frac{FH}{DF}=\frac{FH}{(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{\sin (180-\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75})})}=\frac{FH\sin (180-\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75})}{\sqrt{3}}=\frac{FH(\sqrt{3})\sin (180-\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75})}{3}$; having already deduced that m$\angle CDF=\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75}-60$, $\sin(\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75}-60)=\frac{FH(\sqrt{3})\sin (180-\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75})}{3} \Rightarrow 3\sin(\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75}-60)=FH(\sqrt{3})\sin (180-\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75}) \Rightarrow FH=\frac{3\sin(\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75}-60)}{(\sqrt{3})\sin (180-\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75})}$. The triangle area formula is $\frac{1}{2}bh$ where $b$ is the length of its base and $h$ is the length of its height (8); its application upon $\triangle CDF$ yields $\frac{1}{2}\cdot 2\cdot (\frac{3\sin(\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75}-60)}{(\sqrt{3})\sin (180-\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75})})= \boxed{\frac{3\sin(\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75}-60)}{(\sqrt{3})\sin (180-\tan^{-1} 2\sqrt{6.75})}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3} \text{  in}^2}$
		
		\end{quotation}
